How to Make Extraordinary Coffee: Using a French Press

Master the French press method for a rich, full-bodied cup of Kaawa Mpologoma.

Home / Our Blog / French Press Guide

What You Need

  • A French press (any size)
  • Freshly ground Kaawa Mpologoma coffee (coarse grind)
  • Hot water (just off the boil — about 93°C / 200°F)
  • A timer

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the French Press

Remove the lid and plunger from the French press. Pre-warm the glass by rinsing it with hot water, then discard that water. This keeps your coffee hotter for longer.

Step 2: Add Coffee Grounds

You will need 2 tablespoons (14 g) of ground coffee for each serving (approximately 200 mL of water). For a standard 350 mL French press, use about 3 tablespoons. Kaawa Mpologoma should be ground coarsely — similar to rough sea salt. A coarse grind prevents over-extraction and keeps the plunger from clogging.

Step 3: Add Hot Water

Pour hot water (just off the boil) over the grounds. Pour in a slow, circular motion to ensure all the grounds are saturated. Fill to about 1 cm below the top of the press. Give it a gentle stir with a wooden or plastic spoon — avoid metal, which can crack the glass.

Step 4: Steep for 4 Minutes

Place the lid on the French press with the plunger pulled all the way up. Start your timer and let the coffee steep for 4 minutes. This is the sweet spot for a balanced, full-bodied cup. Steeping longer will make it stronger and more bitter; shorter will make it weaker.

Step 5: Press and Pour

After 4 minutes, press the plunger down slowly and steadily with even pressure. If it's very hard to press, your grind is too fine. If it drops with no resistance, your grind is too coarse. Pour immediately into your cup — don't leave the coffee sitting in the press or it will continue to extract and become bitter.

Tips for the Best Cup

  • Always use a coarse grind for French press. Fine grinds pass through the mesh filter and make the coffee muddy.
  • Water temperature matters — boiling water (100°C) will scorch the grounds. Let it rest 30 seconds after boiling.
  • Pour all the coffee out immediately after pressing. Leaving it in the press continues the extraction.
  • Clean the French press thoroughly after each use — old coffee oils turn rancid and ruin the flavour of your next brew.
  • For a stronger cup, increase the coffee-to-water ratio rather than steeping longer.
