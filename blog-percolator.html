How to Make Extraordinary Coffee: Using a Percolator

A simple, step-by-step guide to getting the best out of your percolator with Kaawa Mpologoma.

Home / Our Blog / Percolator Guide

What You Need

  • A stovetop or electric percolator
  • Freshly ground Kaawa Mpologoma coffee (coarse grind recommended)
  • Filtered or bottled water
  • A heat source

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Add Water

Heat some water, then pour it into the bottom chamber of the percolator. For best results, use filtered or bottled water. Avoid tap water if it has a strong taste or odour, as this will affect the flavour of your coffee.

Step 2: Add Coffee Grounds

Place the basket into the percolator and add your ground coffee. Use approximately 1 tablespoon of coarsely ground coffee per cup of water. Kaawa Mpologoma works beautifully at a medium-coarse grind — it allows the water to cycle through without over-extracting.

Step 3: Assemble and Heat

Assemble the percolator and place it on the heat source. For a stovetop percolator, use medium heat. For an electric percolator, simply switch it on. The water will begin to cycle upward through the tube and over the coffee grounds.

Step 4: Watch the Percolation

Watch the coffee through the glass knob on top (if your percolator has one). Once you see the coffee cycling steadily — a rich, dark colour — reduce the heat slightly. Percolate for 7 to 10 minutes depending on how strong you like your coffee.

Step 5: Remove from Heat and Serve

Remove the percolator from the heat and let it rest for 1 minute before pouring. This allows the grounds to settle. Pour carefully and enjoy your cup of Kaawa Mpologoma.

Tips for the Best Cup

  • Use a coarse grind — fine grinds can pass through the basket and make your coffee gritty.
  • Don't over-percolate. More than 10 minutes can make the coffee bitter.
  • Pre-warm your cup with hot water before pouring to keep your coffee hotter for longer.
  • Clean your percolator thoroughly after each use to prevent stale oil build-up.
